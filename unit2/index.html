Which two websites are you most inspired by?

Maaemo.no
Simonfosterdesign.com

How do they exhibit use of space, size, or balance?

Maaemo.no displays an interesting use of columns that create a dynamic relationship between the positive and negative space as you work your way down below the fold. All of the negative space is white. This forces your eye to the contrasting images, which take you to other pages within the site. This web page feels like it was designed to be intentionally imbalanced. The size of the images vary greatly throughout. Whether it is two beside eachother, or one big one across the whole page, the site almost forces your mouse to drag over an image as you scroll, which causes the image to then take on a filter that acts as a visual cue for the user to enter the corresponding page.

Simonfosterdesign.com Uses two balanced columns with one image and one body of text per project displayed. Something that adds interest is how the image and text alternate from right to left on each page as you scroll. I feel like this creates variety while maintaining balance. The negative space in this changes as you scroll down to create a custom screen for each project. Adjusting the styling of the negative space gives a unique touch to each individual project's section of the site. This forces the designer to alter the font color throughout to ensure high contrast of the positive and negative space is maintained with each changing background. According to hierarchical sizing the designer is putting more emphasis on the title of the project, and the link to go see more, while rendering the short description less important.

What makes them similar and different?

Similarities

Both sites are formatted to collapse into a single column as the browser is made smaller or the site is viewed on a mobile device. Each site uses the name of the business, or designer, as the focal point on the first page by way of hierarchy. Both sites are really clean and appealing visually, with minimal information on the homepage. They chose rather to direct you away from the landing page to find the more detailed information you want.

Differences

When on a desktop screen, each site takes advantage of the use of columns in different ways. One leverages a more consistent two column format, while the other has images and text bouncing around from inhabiting two columns seperately to starting in one and crossing over into the next. Simonfosterdesign.com uses a larger variety of color and more than one style of negative space, whereas Maaemo.no keeps it really clean and consistent with white negative space throughout the site.

This entire site will be balanced and as traditional and straight forward as a cool looking site can be because the target client of this business is a fifty year old man with not much tech exposure. Creating a site that makes it easy to find the information the user wants quickly is the main goal. The focal point in this will be the Alberta provincial border outline as opposed to the text. In this industry it is important for people to know we are Albertans, born and bred. Oil men in this region take great pride in being Albertan, we want to identify with that. Hierarcically, this image carries the most visual weight on the page. The about us box will be above the actual description giving it some hierarchical advantage over the body of text, only to make it as easy as possible for user to follow exactly where they are as they work through the site.
